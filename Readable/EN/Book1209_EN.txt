<i>(Strange characters bleed across the surface of the stone tablet. The words make sense to you, though you can't say why.)</i>

— Were the ancient capital of old entombed while it stood,
Death would not have found it on the breeze.

Vanquished exile of a fallen kingdom, Knight of the Golden Bough, wanderer without a hearth;
To cold ash he turned amidst the grief of his beloved,
Never to unseal the ruined city's gates from this side of the world again.

<i>(A message from Istoria emerges under the text...)</i>

"I have no idea who wrote the display boards around here, and I haven't the faintest clue what they're going on about. But anyway, it looks like every door in this exhibition hall only opens from the inside. If Arcadian city-states were all designed like this, then their downfall really isn't that surprising."
