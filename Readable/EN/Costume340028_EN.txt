Back in the days when she could still claim to be a "young girl," Citlali had already displayed an extraordinary talent for shamanic arts. Yet, instead of carrying those gifts to the altar of the Masters of the Night-Wind, she chose to pursue the quiet comforts of a leisurely life.
The techniques that would have left master weavers in awe were used by her to craft soft, flowing skirts. The secret arts that would have stunned tribal priests were used to blend enchanting colors. In the end, what bloomed from these transcendent talents was a set of garments the young girl holds dear.
Some of her elders had once tactfully suggested that such an outfit wasn't quite suitable for wearing out in public, which, in truth, was exactly what Citlali had intended. To her, so long as the snacks were plentiful, her bedroom was the only world that mattered. On the day she first put on her new attire, she turned away every visitor, savoring her solitude as a young girl before eventually drifting off to sleep in her clothes.
Yet, even as this attire offered Citlali a deep and restful slumber, it drew the attention of two ancient gazes. Beneath the shimmering starlight, the enigmatic mists lingering in the breeze drifted into her dreams. In her daze, she seemed to catch the faint echoes of a conversation between the Lord of the Night and the Wayob...
"In her hands, the colored mists are spun into fine thread, and the starry sky is imprinted into patterns; her worthiness is beyond doubt. Bestow your blessing upon her — may the long years she traverses serve to sustain this ancient mandate."
"And yet, in the futures I foresee, she remains as she is now: solitary and alone... How, then, shall she endure the vast expanse of time?"
"To bear the burden of history is a path destined for solitude."
Then, the night wind swept the enigmatic mists away, and the stars ceased their shimmering.
...
In these days, with everyone addressing her as "Granny," Citlali still occasionally pulls out this attire, reminiscing about the dream from that night. Since ascending to the rank of Great Shaman, tribal matters have inevitably found their way to her door, forcing her into formal shamanic dress and away from the small sanctuary of her bedroom. Thus, like her maiden days, this outfit gradually drifted to the periphery of her life.
Yet, a sliver of that maidenly willful spirit has managed to traverse centuries, remaining tucked away in the depths of her heart. Every so often, Citlali changes into these clothes once more, accenting them with a few trendy accessories to keep the look current. In these moments, she casts all official duties aside, hanging a "Do Not Disturb" sign on her door or heading out for a stroll. Should someone hopelessly oblivious dare to pester her, they would likely find themselves knocked into next week.
