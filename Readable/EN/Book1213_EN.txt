—Note—

(Written in two languages. The language used in the upper text is completely unfamiliar.)

Your containment facility is rather ingenious. It took me years of careful tinkering from the inside before I finally managed to slip out, though I trust that will not shock you too much. I may only be a novelist, but I have wandered across many worlds and devoured countless mystery and detective stories, so a sharp mind is practically part of the job.

If you happen to find this note, do relax. The containment vessel should still be intact. I think.

To be honest, the days locked inside were so unbearably dull that I ended up doing something rather productive. I finally worked out the ending to VM. Even so, I must say that what you did was rather unfair. The universe may not share a single code of laws, but locking someone away without so much as an explanation seems a little unreasonable, does it not? You did not even allow the border authorities to speak with me.

So now you tell me I entered illegally. That is rather amusing, given that I have already received royalties in your local currency. By now the publisher (Yae Publishing House, if I have the name right), must have readers searching everywhere for me.

Then again, after all these years, I doubt anyone still cares how the story ends.

This message was written in two languages (the language of the Andromeda Empire and the local tongue). The two scripts are printed together to reduce the chance of misunderstanding.
