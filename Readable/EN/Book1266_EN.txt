Today, I will bid farewell to Professor Maagees. In accordance with her wishes, I will perform that operation of creation on her later today, just as she once did when she created Ungie.
This is something I have to do myself, for it is what Professor Maagees wants. The experience I gain from the operation will be vital to the upcoming Project Moongazer, particularly to the work of creating life itself. This is also in line with King Xiuhcoatl's wise counsel.
For so long, I believed her insistence on sending that message was simply grief turned into obsession, the fixation of a human being who had lost the one she loved.
But then she showed me the entirety of the message brought by the Stellar Blackbody. It was only then that I finally understood what she had been reaching for.
I understand now, too, why the Eye of Ibni-Belum team chose to stand with her, even if that meant defying Kalag's security force, and even though to the first Moongazers, both she and Professor Ungien were considered traitors...
The pioneer Ibni-Belum once proclaimed to the researchers who had set foot on the moon: "The sky of stars above our heads was born not of the King's dreams. It hath ever been there, mysterious and silent, awaiting the day its children would look up in curiosity..."
Yes, my teachers were much like children of the stars. They loved the starry sky with such devotion that, from where I stood behind them, I could almost glimpse the shape of the universe in their silhouettes.
...
My dear teachers, it has been my honor to spend this time with you on the moon.
Though you are now gone, your will remains. I, Lusalim, your student, will see it carried forth.
