I should attend this meeting, I told my "self." Truth be told, not a single one of my "selves" occupies a critical position in my world, for the ample proliferation of my myriad selves ensures a perpetual surplus of manpower ready to dispose of whatever trivialities might crop up in this world.
Twenty-five-year-old "me" cast a sidelong glance at me, let out a cold laugh, and walked away. It is impossible to define the principles by which "we" are ordered or ranked, in truth, there is no formal hierarchy between us. However, the mere adoption of the epithet "Doctor" immediately establishes a relationship of superiors and subordinates with the other individuals in the organization.
I headed to that secret meeting, where Pierro explained the details of that so-called "false sky."
To me, the revelation of that false sky is of little consequence — I have no use for it, not while my hands are full of far more pressing research. That being said, the question of what lies beyond that sky is a curiosity worth pondering.
I recorded the details and told Pierro quite frankly that perhaps, one day, these secrets could be exchanged for even more useful commodities. Pierro offered a rather non-committal chuckle, while Pantalone signaled his agreement with my appraisal without saying a single word otherwise. As for Pulcinella, he mumbled a few choice phrases — mostly regarding some confidentiality agreement.
I had no interest in explaining myself further. That day will eventually come, sooner or later.
