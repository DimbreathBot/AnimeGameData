...I have performed some brief deductions. It appears that Professor Maagees... Researcher Maagees-Geti was on the right path after all.

...Mathematically, the conclusion can be formally derived that any system of sufficient complexity is computationally equivalent and possesses an indistinguishable identity in any measurable sense.

...In other words, a self-awareness simulated by a sophisticated mechanical or informational architecture is, by definition, no different from the consciousness of organic life.

...She yearns to reclaim a life from yesterday, yet the result of her realization is the genesis of a life for tomorrow... For her, what could be more ironic?

...

...Researcher Lusalim approached me today with a peculiar proposal.

...Based on their current findings, he claims it is theoretically possible to engrave the full measure of a researcher's affections, desires, and ideals into data. Through the application of (...), these fragments could be manifested as an "immortal life." He does not mean "immortality" in the literal sense — After all, only a madman or a fool devoid of common sense would believe the laws of this universe permit the existence of an eternal, flawless object. Rather, he envisions a life that can re-emerge, infinitely, no matter how many times it is extinguished. Even if its new incarnation no longer bears the form of a dragon...

...In this way, he argues, the ideals of the Moongazers, and the aspirations of Professor Maagees could be broadcast across the ages, just like a certain beam of light drifting through the void for billions of years.

...He intends to name this unborn life "Nin-Kiggia," or messenger. Well, I bet it's nothing more than a whim of pure sentiment.

...I told him plainly that I have no interest. I don't even understand why he chose to confide in me. Are we truly so familiar? Besides, I am a recorder, not a researcher...

...

King Xiuhcoatl paid a visit today. His Highness, with his characteristic gentleness, expressed a profound interest in Researcher Maagees-Geti's theories, noting their parallels to his own ongoing work. He hopes to manifest life through the use of Phlogiston (or what we call computronium), so that our kin upon the surface might find some small relief, and perhaps, a measure of happiness.

...I spoke with the King at length, and I must admit the conversation was most enlightening. However, in a moment of carelessness, I mentioned Researcher Lusalim's plan to him. After a moment's reflection, His Highness offered a wealth of practical, actionable suggestions...

...It truly was a lapse in judgment. I have already begun to regret it. I am a recorder, not a researcher. My exchange with the King was merely a slip of the tongue — I neither can, nor will, nor do I have any desire to provide assistance to Lusalim's project.

...

...Yet, out of professional rigor, I have forwarded the King's suggestions to Researcher Lusalim anyway.
